Heroic - King Rastakhan

Eradication and Internal Combustion is better than Flashover and Reverse Entropy in theory if you are up for the added maintenance of Immolate and Eradication throughout the fight, but most opt to just use FO and RE.

This is a primarily single target multi-phase encounter that begins with 2 adds before the boss, King Rastakhan, becomes attackable. Bwonsamdi spawns once the phase 1 adds die and you can maintain Immolate on him for shards and Flashpoint uptime (although he absorbs all damage). When Rastakhan hits 60% phase 3 begins and much of the raid will go into a death realm (aka "downstairs") to burst Bwonsamdi to 50% hp when phase 4 begins. A couple adds spawn in phase 4 although they are often just CC'd while you burn the boss. Single target talents and traits are generally recommended here, although Flashpoint is extremely powerful for Phase 1 to Phase 3 of this fight as you will likely have close to 100% uptime. As usual your azerite and trinket choices depend on the gear you have available, be sure to sim your character over at Raidbots.

  • When Rastakhan joins the fight in Phase 1, ensure you put Immolate on him for Flashpoint uptime and shards
  • In phase 2 position yourself slightly outside Bwonsamdi's Aura of Death to easily maintain Immolate on him while continuing to single target Rastakhan.
  • Save Infernal and Dark Soul: Instability for when you get to sent down to Bwonsamdi's Death Realm if you have to go down, otherwise save it for lust upstairs.
  • Pet choice is completely irrelevant here so long as it isn't Voidwalker; there are no interrupts, purges, dispels, slows, or target swaps required.
  • Gateway and Circle can be used to maximize casting in phase 4 during Inevitable End.

Mythic - King Rastakhan

The mythic version of this encounter is basically a beefed up version of heroic and very little changes for Destruction.

  • Bwonsamdi's Aura of Death is significantly larger (45 yards) in mythic so dotting him for Flashpoint uptime and shards in phase 2 requires additional movement and risk, however is generally still worthwhile.
